# Expert Product & UX Analysis -- Gamer Wellness

**Project**: Gamer Wellness (working name) -- First gaming+wellness community in LATAM
**Launch market**: Ecuador | **Scale**: LATAM | **Vision**: Global
**Date**: 2026-04-07
**Analyst role**: Senior Product & UX Designer

---

## 1. User Personas

### 1.1 "El Casual" -- Casual Gamer (18-30)

- **Role**: Mobile-first gamer who plays 1-3 hours daily, primarily Free Fire, Call of Duty Mobile, or Minecraft. Lives in Quito or Guayaquil. Likely university student or early-career professional.
- **Goals**: Find local gamers to hang out with IRL; feel less guilty about sedentary gaming time; join low-pressure social events that combine gaming with something active; get free stuff (merch, game credits, food).
- **Pain Points**: Feels isolated despite being in multiple Discord servers; knows gaming posture is destroying their back but has no structured alternative; most wellness content feels preachy, clinical, or targeted at non-gamers; 87% of their gaming is on mobile but "wellness apps" assume they have Apple Watch or expensive wearables.
- **Context**: Discovers the platform via TikTok or Instagram Reel showing a "Caminata Dota" meetup clip. Joins Discord first. Uses phone for everything. Bandwidth may be inconsistent (mobile data, not always Wi-Fi). Spanish-dominant, reads English gaming terms naturally.
- **Jobs to Be Done**: "Help me meet people who get me, without making it feel like a support group." "Give me a reason to move that doesn't feel like homework."

### 1.2 "El Competitivo" -- Competitive/Hardcore Gamer (16-28)

- **Role**: Dedicated PC or console player. Plays Valorant, League of Legends, Dota 2, or fighting games at ranked/tournament level. May aspire to or be part of a local esports team (Noire, Raven, SkullCracker). 40-60+ hours per week of gameplay.
- **Goals**: Improve performance through better physical and mental health (reaction time, focus, tilt control); track metrics that prove wellness changes improve gameplay; compete in wellness leaderboards that respect the competitive mindset; get recognized by brands and teams.
- **Pain Points**: 38.3% high burnout risk; 37% depression/anxiety symptoms; sleeps under 8 hours consistently; has tried generic fitness apps but abandoned them in under 2 weeks; feels that wellness programs don't understand the intensity of competitive gaming; sees "take a break" messaging as condescending.
- **Context**: Already in multiple Discord servers. High engagement with Twitch and YouTube. Uses PC primarily, phone secondarily. Will download a dedicated app if the value proposition is performance-linked. Likely has above-average bandwidth (needs it for competitive play).
- **Jobs to Be Done**: "Help me perform better by being healthier -- show me the data." "Give me a community that takes competition AND wellness seriously."

### 1.3 "La Madre/Padre Gamer" -- Gamer Parent (28-45)

- **Role**: Parent who games recreationally (1-2 hours after kids sleep) or has gaming children (ages 8-16). May be a former hardcore gamer who "aged out" of the competitive scene. Likely in a professional career. Concerned about their own and their children's health habits around gaming.
- **Goals**: Find family-friendly gaming events; model healthy gaming habits for their kids; connect with other parents who understand gaming culture (vs. parents who demonize screens); access evidence-based information about gaming and child development; participate in wellness challenges that fit a parent's schedule.
- **Pain Points**: Feels caught between gaming culture (which often ignores health) and parenting culture (which often demonizes gaming); no Spanish-language resources that take a balanced, evidence-based view; meetup events skew young and single; guilt about their own screen time compounding with worry about kids' screen time.
- **Context**: Discovers via Facebook Group, WhatsApp group share, or Instagram. Values structured, safe, well-organized events. Will use the app for family wellness tracking. Likely has stable home Wi-Fi but limited personal time for engagement.
- **Jobs to Be Done**: "Help me raise healthy gamers -- including myself." "Connect me with parents who get it."

### 1.4 "El Lider" -- Community Leader / Moderator (20-35)

- **Role**: Natural organizer within the gaming community. May already moderate a Discord server or organize informal LAN parties. Passionate about both gaming and helping others. Could evolve into a club leader, event organizer, or peer wellness coach.
- **Goals**: Lead a meetup group or club with real structure and support; gain leadership skills and credentials they can put on a resume; access moderation tools and training (especially for mental health conversations); earn recognition, XP, and tangible rewards for community contributions; become a paid community manager or event organizer long-term.
- **Pain Points**: Burns out from unpaid community labor; lacks training for handling mental health crises in chat; has no framework for turning a casual gaming group into a sustainable club; feels undervalued by platforms that profit from volunteer moderation.
- **Context**: Power user of Discord. Active across multiple platforms. Willing to use dedicated tools and dashboards. Needs mobile access for event day logistics but also uses desktop for planning. Expects real-time communication channels.
- **Jobs to Be Done**: "Give me the tools and recognition to build something meaningful." "Help me help my community without burning out."

### 1.5 "La Marca / Aseguradora" -- B2B Partner (Insurance, Healthcare, Brand)

- **Role**: Marketing director, innovation lead, or partnership manager at an insurer (Aseguradora del Sur), hospital (Hospital de los Valles), telecom (Tigo), or consumer brand (Visa, Samsung, Red Bull). Needs to reach the 18-35 demographic that ignores traditional advertising.
- **Goals**: Access a qualified, consented audience of gamers with behavioral and health-adjacent data; activate campaigns (sponsorships, challenges, branded events) with measurable ROI; understand gaming audience segments for product development (insurance products, health programs, telecom bundles); demonstrate innovation/CSR in board presentations.
- **Pain Points**: Traditional advertising to gamers has low ROI (ad-blockers, banner blindness); no existing platform in LATAM offers consented gamer health/behavioral data; insurer sales cycles are 12-24 months -- needs quick proof of concept; compliance burden is high (LOPDP) and any data scandal would be career-ending.
- **Context**: Accesses a B2B dashboard on desktop. Needs clean, exportable reports. Cares about audience size, engagement metrics, demographic breakdowns, and consent audit trails. Evaluates based on CPM/CPA benchmarks from traditional digital advertising. Monthly or quarterly reporting cadence.
- **Jobs to Be Done**: "Show me an audience I can't reach anywhere else, with data I can trust is compliant." "Make me look innovative to my board."

### 1.6 "La Gamer Mujer" -- Female Gamer (16-30) [Supplementary Persona]

- **Role**: Female gamer in a region where 90% of casual women gamers report gender-based harassment. Plays across genres but may hide gender identity in competitive spaces. Active in smaller, trusted communities.
- **Goals**: Find a safe, moderated space where gender is not the defining characteristic of her experience; participate in events where she feels physically safe; access wellness resources that acknowledge the specific mental health toll of harassment; see representation in leadership and content.
- **Pain Points**: Harassment is the norm, not the exception; most gaming communities treat "women's issues" as a sidebar rather than core design; mental health impacts of harassment are compounded by lack of culturally relevant support in Spanish; IRL events feel risky without clear safety protocols.
- **Context**: May lurk before participating. Values strong moderation policies that are visibly enforced. Will evaluate the community based on how it handles the first incident she witnesses. Mobile-first. Active on Instagram and TikTok.
- **Jobs to Be Done**: "Let me be a gamer first, not a 'girl gamer'." "Show me this space is actually safe before I invest my time."

## 2. User Journeys

### 2.1 Discovery to Active Member

**Trigger**: User sees a TikTok/Instagram Reel of a "Caminata Dota" meetup -- people in gaming merch hiking together, laughing, with overlay text: "Gamers que se mueven."

| Step | User Action | System / Platform Response |
|------|------------|---------------------------|
| 1. **Discovery** | Watches short-form video. Taps bio link or searches handle. | Link-in-bio directs to a mobile-optimized landing page (not Discord directly). Landing page shows: next 3 events, "Join the community" CTA, 15-second highlight reel. Language auto-detected (ES default, EN toggle). |
| 2. **Discord Join** | Clicks "Unete al Discord" from landing page. | Discord invite link opens. Bot greets with welcome message in ES (EN option). Onboarding channel presents: (a) select your city [Quito/Guayaquil/Cuenca/Otro], (b) select your main game [curated list + "Otro"], (c) agree to community guidelines. User gets roles assigned automatically. |
| 3. **Orientation** | Browses channels. Reads #bienvenida. Sees #proximos-eventos pinned post. | Bot sends DM after 24h: "Hey! Hay un meetup de [game] en [city] el [date]. Quieres que te avise?" Links to event details on the Web platform (not in Discord). |
| 4. **First Meetup** | RSVPs on Web platform (no account required -- just name + phone for WhatsApp reminder). Attends event. | At event: check-in via QR code scan (opens Web app). First-time attendees get a physical "Welcome Pack" card with QR linking to app registration. Post-event: WhatsApp message with event photos + "Crea tu perfil para ganar XP por haber asistido." |
| 5. **App Onboarding** | Scans QR or follows WhatsApp link. Downloads app or opens PWA. | Registration flow: (a) "Link your Discord" via Discord OAuth (1-click, pre-fills username + avatar), (b) basic profile (display name, city, main games -- pre-filled from Discord roles), (c) consent screen v1: "Compartir datos basicos de perfil para personalizar tu experiencia" [Accept/Decline details]. Total onboarding: under 90 seconds. |
| 6. **Data Consent** | Sees consent prompt AFTER first value moment (XP from event attendance credited). | Granular consent screen: checkboxes (not bundled) for (a) profile data for personalization, (b) event attendance history, (c) wellness activity data, (d) anonymized data for research, (e) anonymized data shared with partners [each partner category listed]. Each toggle independent. "Puedes cambiar esto en cualquier momento en Configuracion." |
| 7. **Active Member** | Checks app weekly for events, challenges, leaderboard position. Engages in Discord daily. | App surfaces: upcoming events, active wellness challenges, XP/level progress, community leaderboard. Discord bot posts weekly recaps linking to app content. Push notifications (opt-in) for events in user's city and game. |

- **Success Criteria**: User attends 3+ events in first 2 months; creates app profile; grants at least basic consent tier; returns to app weekly.
- **Drop-off Risks**:
  - Step 2: Discord onboarding too many steps -- keep to 3 taps max.
  - Step 4-5: Gap between physical event and digital onboarding -- the QR/WhatsApp bridge is critical.
  - Step 6: Consent screen feels invasive too early -- must come AFTER first value delivery (XP credit).
  - Step 7: App has no compelling reason to return if user only cares about events -- need event-independent value (challenges, content, social features).

### 2.2 Event Lifecycle

**Trigger**: User is an active Discord member who sees a new event announcement.

| Step | User Action | System / Platform Response |
|------|------------|---------------------------|
| 1. **Event Discovery** | Sees event announcement in Discord #eventos channel or push notification from app. | Event card shows: name, theme (game tie-in), date/time, location (map link), capacity, current RSVPs (social proof: "12 gamers van"), difficulty level (Chill/Activo/Intenso), what to bring. |
| 2. **Registration** | Taps "Me apunto" on Web/App. | If logged in: 1-tap RSVP. If not: lightweight registration (name + phone). Confirmation via WhatsApp + calendar invite (.ics). Reminder 24h and 2h before event. Waitlist if at capacity with auto-notify. |
| 3. **Pre-Event** | Receives WhatsApp reminder with location pin, what to expect, and "Opcional: activa tu wearable para trackear tu actividad durante el evento." | App shows event detail page with: attendee avatars (opt-in), route map (for outdoor events), weather forecast, suggested prep (hydration, shoes, sunscreen for outdoor). |
| 4. **Check-in** | Arrives at venue. Scans QR code displayed at entrance (or shows QR from app to organizer). | Check-in registers attendance. Awards "Attendance XP." If wearable connected: begins activity tracking session. First-timer: welcome interaction from designated "host" community leader. |
| 5. **Participation** | Engages in the activity (hike, run, chill zone, food meetup, tournament). | If wearable active: real-time stats available (steps, heart rate zone, calories -- visible only to user). Photo opportunities with branded frames (shareable to stories). Mid-event challenge (optional): "Gana 50 XP extra: completa 3,000 pasos antes de que termine." |
| 6. **XP & Rewards** | Event ends. Checks app for XP summary. | Post-event screen: XP breakdown (attendance + activity + bonus challenges + streak bonus). Level-up animation if threshold crossed. Badge earned ("Caminata Dota -- Primera vez"). Comparison: "Caminaste 4.2km -- equivalente a cruzar el mapa de Summoner's Rift 47 veces." |
| 7. **Post-Event Social** | Shares event badge/stats to Instagram Stories or Discord. Views event photos in app gallery. Leaves optional feedback. | Auto-generated shareable card (dark theme, gaming aesthetic, personal stats + event branding). Discord bot posts event recap in #recaps channel with group photo + aggregate stats. Feedback form: 3 questions max (NPS, favorite moment, suggestion). |

- **Success Criteria**: 70%+ of RSVPs attend; 50%+ check in digitally; 30%+ share post-event content; event NPS > 8.
- **Drop-off Risks**:
  - Step 2: Registration friction -- phone-only signup must be available for non-app users.
  - Step 4: QR check-in fails (poor connectivity at outdoor venues) -- offline fallback needed (manual check-in by organizer with sync later).
  - Step 6: XP feels meaningless if not connected to tangible progress or recognition.

### 2.3 Wellness Challenge Flow

**Trigger**: User sees a new weekly challenge in the app or via Discord bot announcement.

| Step | User Action | System / Platform Response |
|------|------------|---------------------------|
| 1. **Challenge Discovery** | Opens app "Challenges" tab or sees Discord bot post: "Nuevo desafio: Semana del Movimiento -- Camina 30,000 pasos en 7 dias. Recompensa: 500 XP + badge exclusivo." | Challenge card: name, description (gaming-themed narrative), duration, goal, reward, current participants, difficulty tier (Bronce/Plata/Oro = different targets for same challenge). |
| 2. **Enrollment** | Taps "Acepto el desafio." | If wearable not connected: prompt to connect (Apple Health / Health Connect / Open Wearables). If no wearable: manual entry option available ("Registra tu actividad manualmente"). Clear explanation: "Tus datos de actividad se usan solo para este desafio. Puedes desconectar en cualquier momento." |
| 3. **Wearable Sync** | Grants health data permission on device. | App requests ONLY step count + active minutes (not heart rate, sleep, or other sensitive data -- those require separate, explicit consent). Background sync begins. Confirmation: "Conectado. Tus pasos se actualizan cada hora." |
| 4. **Progress Tracking** | Checks app daily to see progress bar and position. | Dashboard shows: personal progress (X/30,000 steps), daily breakdown chart, position in challenge leaderboard (opt-in to leaderboard visibility), motivational message tied to gaming: "Vas por el 60% -- como llegar a la torre enemiga del mid lane." |
| 5. **Leaderboard** | Taps leaderboard tab. Sees friends and community members ranked. | Leaderboard shows: top 50, user's position, friends highlighted. Filters: global / city / game community. Anonymous option: user can participate in challenge without appearing on leaderboard (still earns XP). Anti-cheat: anomaly detection flags unrealistic step counts for review. |
| 6. **Completion & Reward** | Finishes challenge. Receives notification. | Celebration screen with animation (level-up style). Badge added to profile. XP credited. If Oro tier reached: bonus reward (could be merch, event priority access, or partner voucher). Shareable completion card generated. |
| 7. **Streak & Retention** | Completes multiple challenges over weeks. | Streak counter visible on profile. Streak bonuses (XP multiplier). Monthly "Challenge Recap" with aggregate stats. Suggested next challenge based on history. Community comparison: "Este mes, la comunidad camino un total de 2.3 millones de pasos -- equivalente a recorrer la Ruta de los Volcanes 18 veces." |

- **Success Criteria**: 40%+ of enrolled users complete the challenge; 20%+ connect a wearable; week-over-week challenge enrollment grows.
- **Drop-off Risks**:
  - Step 2: Wearable requirement as barrier -- manual entry must be a first-class option, not an afterthought.
  - Step 4: No mid-challenge engagement -- need daily nudges (not spammy; gaming-themed, opt-in).
  - Step 5: Leaderboard discourages casual users who can't compete -- tiered difficulty (Bronce/Plata/Oro) solves this.
  - Step 6: Reward feels hollow -- tangible rewards (even small: stickers, Discord role) matter more than XP alone.

### 2.4 B2B Partner Dashboard Flow

**Trigger**: Partnership manager at Aseguradora del Sur receives login credentials after commercial agreement is signed.

| Step | User Action | System / Platform Response |
|------|------------|---------------------------|
| 1. **Dashboard Login** | Navigates to partners.gamerwellness.ec. Logs in with SSO or email/password + MFA. | Dashboard home: overview metrics (total consented audience size, active users this month, event attendance, engagement rate). Clear badge: "Datos basados en [N] usuarios con consentimiento explicito verificado." Compliance timestamp visible. |
| 2. **Audience Insights** | Explores audience segments. Filters by: age range, city, game preference, activity level, engagement tier, event attendance frequency. | Visualizations: demographic breakdowns, behavioral segments (e.g., "Active Movers" = users who complete 3+ challenges/month; "Social Connectors" = users who attend 2+ events/month; "Competitive Core" = ranked players with high engagement). All data aggregated -- no individual-level data exposed. Minimum cohort size enforced (e.g., n >= 50) to prevent de-anonymization. |
| 3. **Campaign Activation** | Creates a campaign: selects audience segment, campaign type (sponsored challenge, branded event, in-app offer, survey), budget, timeline. | Campaign builder wizard: (a) select segment, (b) choose activation type, (c) set parameters (challenge goals, reward budget, event details), (d) preview how it appears to users, (e) submit for review. All campaigns require Gamer Wellness editorial approval before going live (brand safety + community trust). |
| 4. **Campaign Performance** | Monitors live campaign metrics. | Real-time dashboard: impressions, engagement rate, challenge completions, event RSVPs, survey responses, sentiment analysis (from optional feedback). Comparison to benchmarks: "Tu challenge tuvo 3.2x mas completions que el promedio de la plataforma." |
| 5. **Reporting & Export** | Downloads monthly or campaign report. | PDF/CSV export with: methodology notes, consent audit summary, LOPDP compliance statement, aggregate metrics. No raw data export -- only aggregated insights. API available for enterprise partners to integrate with their BI tools (rate-limited, audited). |
| 6. **Renewal / Expansion** | Reviews ROI. Discusses expansion with Gamer Wellness partnership team. | Dashboard shows: historical performance across campaigns, audience growth trend, suggested new segments or activation types. Account manager contact and meeting scheduler embedded. |

- **Success Criteria**: Partner logs in weekly; activates at least 1 campaign per quarter; renewal rate > 80%; zero compliance incidents.
- **Drop-off Risks**:
  - Step 1: Dashboard is confusing or slow -- must be clean, fast, Metabase-powered with clear data dictionary.
  - Step 2: Audience size too small in early phases -- set expectations during sales; show growth trajectory.
  - Step 3: Campaign approval process too slow -- SLA of 48h for review.
  - Step 5: Partner wants individual-level data -- firm "no" with clear legal rationale; offer deeper aggregate cuts instead.

## 3. Feature Prioritization

### Value vs Effort Matrix

Features are organized by phase alignment and assigned priority levels:
- **P0** = Must have for launch (Phase 1: 0-3 months)
- **P1** = Required for community growth (Phase 2: 3-9 months)
- **P2** = Revenue enablement (Phase 3: 9-24 months)
- **P3** = Scale and optimization (24+ months)

| Feature | User Value | Effort | Priority | Phase | Rationale |
|---------|-----------|--------|----------|-------|-----------|
| **Discord server with structured channels + onboarding bot** | High | Low | P0 | 1 | Foundation of community. Low-code setup. Critical for first 100 members. |
| **Mobile-optimized landing page / link-in-bio hub** | High | Low | P0 | 1 | Discovery funnel. Simple static site or single-page app. |
| **Social media presence (TikTok, IG, YouTube)** | High | Medium | P0 | 1 | Discovery engine. Content creation effort is ongoing. |
| **Event listing page (Web)** | High | Low | P0 | 1 | Simple public page showing upcoming events with RSVP. |
| **WhatsApp event reminders** | High | Low | P0 | 1 | LATAM communication standard. WhatsApp Business API or manual. |
| **QR check-in for events** | Medium | Low | P0 | 1 | Bridges physical to digital. Simple QR code generation + scan page. |
| **Basic Web/PWA with user registration** | High | Medium | P1 | 2 | Required before any data collection. Discord OAuth for frictionless signup. |
| **Discord account linking (OAuth)** | High | Medium | P1 | 2 | Critical bridge between Discord identity and App profile. Uses Discord "1-Click" account linking. |

| **Sponsored challenges / branded activations** | High (B2B) | Medium | P2 | 3 | Primary B2B activation format. Template system for partners. |
| **Advanced analytics pipeline (PostHog + TimescaleDB)** | Medium | High | P2 | 3 | Required for B2B data quality. Self-hosted for LOPDP compliance. |
| **Premium memberships** | Medium | Medium | P2 | 3 | Tiered access: priority event registration, exclusive challenges, cosmetic profile items. |
| **Native mobile app (React Native)** | High | High | P2 | 3 | PWA works for Phase 1-2. Native app needed for deep wearable integration + push reliability. |
| **Multi-country expansion (Colombia, Mexico, Peru)** | High | High | P3 | 4 | Requires localized events, compliance per country, regional community managers. |
| **Insurance gamification integration** | High (B2B) | Very High | P3 | 4 | Discovery Vitality-style program. Requires insurer partnership + complex data pipeline. |
| **NASEF-style wellness certification** | Medium | High | P3 | 4 | B2B2C revenue. Requires curriculum development, credentialing partner. |
| **API for enterprise partners** | Medium (B2B) | High | P3 | 4 | Enables deep integration with insurer/brand systems. Rate-limited, audited. |

## 4. Information Architecture

### 4.1 Discord Server Structure

The Discord server is the community heartbeat. It must NOT collect data, host consent flows, or serve gamification mechanics beyond basic role assignment. Its job is to be the best gaming+wellness community space in LATAM.

```
GAMER WELLNESS (Discord Server)
|
+-- INFORMACION
|   +-- #bienvenida              (Read-only. Rules, vision, links to Web/App)
|   +-- #reglas                  (Code of conduct, moderation policy, safety resources)
|   +-- #roles                   (Self-assign: city, main game, language, pronouns)
|   +-- #faq                     (Bot-maintained FAQ with common questions)
|   +-- #anuncios                (Read-only. Official announcements)
|
+-- COMUNIDAD
|   +-- #general                 (Main chat, Spanish default)
|   +-- #english                 (English-language general chat)
|   +-- #presentaciones          (Introduce yourself)
|   +-- #memes-y-clips           (Media sharing, lighter tone)
|   +-- #fuera-del-juego         (Off-topic: music, food, life)
|
+-- JUEGOS (category per major game cluster)
|   +-- #valorant
|   +-- #league-of-legends
|   +-- #free-fire
|   +-- #dota2
|   +-- #minecraft
|   +-- #fighting-games
|   +-- #mobile-gaming
|   +-- #otros-juegos
|
+-- BIENESTAR
|   +-- #movimiento              (Fitness tips, exercise, stretching routines)

|   +-- #nutricion               (Gamer-friendly nutrition, recipes, meal prep)

+-- CLUBES (created as meetups reach critical mass)
|   +-- #club-caminata-dota

|   +-- #club-chill-minecraft

+-- LIDERAZGO (restricted access)
|   +-- #moderadores             (Mod coordination)

|   +-- #feedback-interno        (Internal feedback and improvement ideas)
|   +-- #crisis-protocol         (Mental health escalation channel -- trained mods only)
|
+-- VOZ / VOICE
|   +-- General Voice
|   +-- Gaming Sessions (multiple)
|   +-- Chill / Music
|   +-- Club Meetings
|   +-- Events Live (for hybrid events)
```

**Key Discord Design Decisions**:
- All event RSVPs, wellness tracking, and data collection link OUT to the Web/App. Discord is the town square; the App is the gym, office, and health center.
- Bot commands: `/evento` (shows next events with Web link), `/perfil` (shows app profile link), `/desafio` (shows active challenges with app link), `/ayuda` (mental health resources + crisis line).
- Moderation: AutoMod configured for hate speech, harassment, self-harm content. Human mods for nuance. 1 mod per 100 members target.
- Teen-appropriate mode compliance (Discord March 2026 default): all content in all channels must be compatible.

## 5. UX Recommendations

### 5.1 Consent Flows -- The Most Critical UX Challenge

The LOPDP (Ecuador's data protection law) requires explicit, granular, individually revocable consent for sensitive data (which includes health data). Fines are real: LigaPro was fined $259K and FEF $195K in January 2026. This is not theoretical risk.

**Recommendation: Progressive Consent Model**

Do NOT present a wall of consent toggles at registration. Instead, layer consent requests at the moment of value delivery:

- **Layer 0 -- Account Creation**: Minimal consent: email, display name, city. Purpose: "Para crear tu cuenta y mostrarte eventos en tu ciudad." One checkbox. This is not sensitive data.

- **Layer 1 -- After First Event (value delivered)**: "Quieres que guardemos tu historial de eventos para ganar XP?" Toggle: event attendance tracking. Purpose clearly stated.

- **Layer 2 -- Before First Challenge Enrollment**: "Para participar en desafios, necesitamos acceso a tus datos de actividad." Granular toggles: (a) step count, (b) active minutes. Purpose: "Solo para trackear tu progreso en desafios." Separate from any sharing.

- **Layer 3 -- Optional Wellness Profile**: "Quieres crear un perfil de bienestar mas completo?" Additional toggles: (a) self-reported health data, (b) sleep data from wearable, (c) heart rate data. Purpose: "Para darte insights personalizados." Still NOT shared with anyone.

- **Layer 4 -- Partner Data Sharing (only after established trust)**: "Nuestros aliados ofrecen beneficios exclusivos. Quieres que compartamos datos anonimizados sobre tu actividad?" Per-category toggles: (a) insurers, (b) healthcare providers, (c) fitness brands, (d) food/beverage brands, (e) tech/telecom. Each with plain-language explanation of what is shared (aggregated segments, never individual data) and why (to fund the free community).

**Consent UI Specifications**:
- Every toggle shows: what data, why, who sees it, how to revoke.
- Consent can be changed at ANY time from Settings > Privacy & Consent.
- Revoking consent triggers immediate cessation of data use (not just future collection).
- "Download my data" and "Delete my account and all data" buttons prominently accessible.
- Consent log visible to user: "El 15 de marzo aceptaste compartir datos de actividad. El 20 de abril revocaste el acceso para aseguradoras."
- Language: plain Spanish. No legalese. Short sentences. Icons for each data type.
- Consent version tracking: if terms change, re-consent required (not passive acceptance).

### 5.2 Discord-to-App Bridge Design

This is the highest-friction transition in the entire product. A user who is happily chatting in Discord has zero motivation to download an app unless the bridge is seamless and the value is immediate.

**Bridge Mechanisms (ranked by priority)**:

1. **Discord OAuth Account Linking**: Users link their Discord account to their App profile with one click. Uses Discord's native Account Linking flow. Pre-fills display name, avatar, and game roles from Discord. This is the primary identity bridge.

2. **Discord Bot as Traffic Driver**: The bot never replicates app functionality in Discord. Instead, it teases value that requires the app:
   - `/mis-puntos` --> "Tienes 350 XP! Ve tu perfil completo: [app link]"
   - `/desafio` --> "Desafio activo: Semana del Movimiento. Inscribete: [app link]"
   - `/evento [city]` --> "3 eventos en Quito esta semana. Detalles y RSVP: [app link]"
   - After event check-in: Bot posts in #fotos-y-recaps: "@usuario completo la Caminata Dota y gano 200 XP! Ve su badge: [app link]"

3. **QR Code at Physical Events**: Every event has a QR code that opens the app check-in page. This is the single most effective conversion moment because the user is physically present, socially engaged, and primed for action.

4. **WhatsApp as Middleware**: For users who resist app download, WhatsApp serves as a lightweight bridge: event reminders, post-event XP notifications, and periodic "Tu resumen semanal" messages that link to the app.

5. **Discord Activities API (Future)**: When mature, embed lightweight wellness activities directly in Discord voice channels (e.g., a stretch-break timer during gaming sessions, a quick mood check-in). These activities link to the full app for tracking and rewards.

**Anti-patterns to Avoid**:
- Do NOT make Discord feel like a second-class experience to push app downloads.
- Do NOT gate community participation behind app registration.
- Do NOT use Discord DMs for marketing (users will leave).
- Do NOT collect any data through Discord bots -- all data collection happens on the App.

### 5.3 Gamification That Feels Authentic

Gamers can smell fake gamification instantly. "Spin the wheel!" and "Collect stars!" will be mocked and rejected. The gamification system must respect gaming culture.

**Design Principles**:

- **Use real gaming vocabulary**: XP (experience points), not "points." Levels, not "tiers." Badges that reference actual game achievements, not corporate clip art. Leaderboards, not "rankings."
- **Progression must feel earned**: XP is gained through meaningful actions (attending events, completing challenges, contributing to community), not passive engagement (logging in, reading content).
- **Multiple progression paths**: Not everyone is competitive. Offer parallel tracks: Fitness (movement challenges), Social (event attendance, community contributions), Knowledge (wellness content engagement), Leadership (moderating, organizing, mentoring).
- **Difficulty tiers for challenges**: Bronce / Plata / Oro -- inspired by ranked tiers in competitive games. A casual gamer can feel accomplished completing Bronce; a competitive gamer has a target in Oro. Both earn XP proportional to difficulty.
- **Gaming-native stat comparisons**: "Caminaste 4.2km -- equivalente a cruzar el mapa de Summoner's Rift 47 veces" or "Tu frecuencia cardiaca en la carrera fue 145 bpm -- la misma que un pro player de Valorant en clutch round."
- **Seasonal events and battle-pass style progression**: Monthly or quarterly themed seasons (like game seasons/battle passes) with exclusive badges and rewards. Creates urgency and renewal.
- **No pay-to-win**: Since gamers never pay, premium memberships should offer cosmetic/convenience benefits (custom profile themes, priority event registration, exclusive Discord roles), never XP boosts or leaderboard advantages.
- **Anti-cheat**: Anomaly detection for wellness data (nobody walks 100,000 steps in a day). Transparent enforcement: "Detectamos datos inusuales. Tu registro fue pausado para revision." Gamers respect anti-cheat systems.

**XP Economy (suggested starting values)**:

| Action | XP | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Attend a meetup | 100 | Base attendance |
| Attend a General Event | 200 | Higher tier event |
| Complete Bronze challenge | 150 | Weekly challenge |
| Complete Silver challenge | 300 | Weekly challenge |
| Complete Gold challenge | 500 | Weekly challenge |
| First event ever | 250 | One-time bonus |
| Streak: 4 consecutive weekly challenges | 200 | Bonus |
| Post in #logros-bienestar (verified) | 50 | Community sharing |
| Refer a friend who attends first event | 150 | Referral |
| Lead/organize a club meetup | 300 | Leader recognition |
| Complete onboarding + consent | 100 | One-time |
| Connect wearable | 100 | One-time |

|-------|------------|-------|

| 2 | 200 | Iniciado |

### 5.4 Mobile-First and Low-Bandwidth Design

Ecuador's median fixed broadband is 77.65 Mbps but mobile experiences vary significantly, especially outside Quito and Guayaquil. 87% of LATAM gamers play on mobile. The web/app must be designed for the weakest common connection.

**Recommendations**:

- **PWA first, native app second**: A Progressive Web App works on any device with a browser, requires no app store download (critical for users with limited phone storage), and can be "installed" to the home screen. Build the PWA in Phase 1-2; launch native React Native app in Phase 3 when wearable integration demands it.
- **Target page load under 3 seconds on 3G**: Lazy-load images, use WebP/AVIF formats, minimize JavaScript bundle size. Server-side render critical content (Next.js SSR).
- **Offline-capable**: Event details, challenge progress, and profile should be cached and viewable offline. Sync when connection returns. Critical for outdoor events (hikes, runs) in areas with poor signal.
- **Data-saver mode**: Optional toggle that disables auto-play videos, reduces image quality, and minimizes background sync frequency. Labeled clearly: "Modo ahorro de datos."
- **SMS fallback for critical communications**: Event reminders and XP notifications should have SMS fallback for users without reliable WhatsApp data.
- **Minimal storage footprint**: PWA target under 5MB. Native app target under 30MB. Many users in Ecuador have 16-32GB phones with limited free space.

### 5.5 Bilingual ES/EN Design

- **Spanish-first, always**: Default language is Spanish. English is a toggle, not a gate. All content must exist in Spanish before English.
- **Gaming terms stay in English**: XP, GG, buff, nerf, clutch, AFK -- these are universal gaming vocabulary in LATAM. Do not translate them. "Ganaste 200 XP" is correct, not "Ganaste 200 puntos de experiencia."
- **UI labels in user's chosen language, gaming terms in English**: The Settings page says "Configuracion" (ES) or "Settings" (EN), but "XP" and "GG" appear in both.
- **Content can be single-language**: Not all articles/videos need both languages. Tag them [ES] or [EN] and filter accordingly. Community-generated content will be overwhelmingly in Spanish.
- **Localization considerations for LATAM expansion**: Use "tu" (not "vos" or "usted") for informal address -- most universally understood across LATAM. Avoid Ecuador-specific slang in UI copy (save it for local event descriptions). Date format: DD/MM/YYYY. Currency: USD (Ecuador) but architecture should support localization.

### 5.6 Safety and Mental Health UX

Given that 37% of competitive players show depression/anxiety symptoms and 90% of women gamers in LATAM report harassment:

- **Visible crisis resources**: A "Necesitas ayuda?" link in the app footer and Discord #recursos channel at all times. Links to Ecuador crisis lines (Linea 171), international resources, and licensed partner (when established).
- **Content warnings on mental health discussions**: In Discord #mente and app Wellness Hub mental health content, a persistent banner: "Este espacio es para compartir y aprender. Si estas en crisis, contacta [crisis resource]. Esto no reemplaza atencion profesional."
- **Report and block**: One-tap reporting in the app for any content or user. In Discord, standard moderation plus trained volunteer mods with clear escalation protocol.
- **Event safety**: All IRL events have a designated "safety contact" -- someone trained in basic crisis response. Events in public, well-lit spaces. Buddy system encouraged for first-time attendees.
- **Moderator mental health**: Mods who handle reports and crisis situations must have access to support themselves. Mandatory rotation to prevent burnout. Monthly check-in with community lead.

## 6. Accessibility Considerations

### 6.1 Language Accessibility
- **Requirement**: Full ES/EN bilingual support across all UI elements, notifications, and onboarding flows.
- **Implementation**: i18n framework from day one (react-intl or i18next). Language stored in user profile, not browser locale (many Ecuadorian users have phones set to English).
- **Content**: Gaming terms remain in English universally. All legal/consent text must be in user's selected language -- LOPDP requires informed consent in a language the user understands.

### 6.2 Mobile-First Design
- **Requirement**: 87%+ of target users are mobile-primary. All features must work fully on mobile screens (360px minimum width).
- **Implementation**: Design mobile layouts first. Desktop is an adaptation, not the primary canvas. Touch targets minimum 44x44px. No hover-dependent interactions.
- **Testing**: Test on mid-range Android devices (Samsung Galaxy A series, Xiaomi Redmi) -- these are the most common phones in Ecuador, not flagships.

### 6.3 Low-Bandwidth and Limited Device Support
- **Requirement**: Functional on 3G connections and phones with 2GB RAM.
- **Implementation**: PWA with offline caching. Lazy loading. No autoplay video. Compressed images with responsive srcset. JavaScript bundle under 200KB gzipped for initial load.
- **Graceful degradation**: If wearable sync fails due to connectivity, queue data locally and sync when connection returns. Show clear "Sincronizando..." state rather than error.

### 6.4 Visual Accessibility
- **Requirement**: WCAG 2.1 AA compliance minimum.
- **Implementation**: Color contrast ratio >= 4.5:1 for text (the dark theme + neon aesthetic must be tested carefully -- neon green on dark gray can fail contrast). Do not use color alone to convey information (leaderboard positions, challenge status). Support system font size scaling up to 200%. Provide alt text for all images including event photos and badges.
- **Dark/Light mode**: Default dark (matches gaming aesthetic and audience preference). Light mode available for outdoor readability (events are outdoors).

### 6.5 Motor Accessibility
- **Requirement**: Usable with one hand on mobile (many users hold phone in one hand while at events).
- **Implementation**: Primary actions reachable with thumb in bottom half of screen. Swipe navigation for common flows (next event, next challenge). No time-limited interactions (except real-time leaderboards which are display-only).

### 6.6 Cognitive Accessibility
- **Requirement**: Consent flows must be understandable by users with varying literacy levels and no legal training.
- **Implementation**: Plain language (Flesch-Kincaid equivalent for Spanish: clear, short sentences). Icons paired with every consent toggle. "What does this mean?" expandable explanation for each consent item. Progress indicators for multi-step flows. Undo capability for all non-destructive actions.

### 6.7 Inclusive Design for Gender Safety
- **Requirement**: Platform must be safe for women and non-binary gamers without requiring them to self-identify.
- **Implementation**: Pronouns are optional in profile (never required). Display names do not require real names. Profile photos optional. Event attendee lists are opt-in visibility. Report mechanisms are prominent and anonymous to the reported user.

## 7. Design System Recommendations

### 7.1 Visual Language

- **Color Palette**: Dark base (#1A1A2E or similar deep navy-charcoal) with primary accent in teal/green (#00F5A0 range -- the intersection of "health" and "HP bar" in gaming). Secondary accents: electric blue (#00D4FF) for interactive elements, warm amber (#FFB800) for XP/rewards, red (#FF4655 -- Valorant red) for alerts/urgent. Avoid pure white backgrounds; use off-white (#F0F0F0) for light mode.
- **Typography**: Bold geometric sans-serif for headings (e.g., Space Grotesk, Exo 2, or Rajdhani -- all free, Latin-extended). Readable humanist sans for body (Inter or Nunito -- both excellent Spanish character support). Monospace for stats/numbers (JetBrains Mono).
- **Iconography**: Custom icon set or consistent library (Phosphor Icons -- supports filled+outline, good gaming feel). Avoid overly corporate icon styles (no Material Design outlined icons for gamification elements).
- **Motion**: Subtle, purposeful animations. Level-up celebrations should feel like in-game achievement unlocks. Loading states should reference gaming (HP bar filling, not generic spinner). Keep animations under 300ms for functional transitions; up to 1.5s for celebrations.
- **Cards**: Rounded corners (12-16px radius), subtle glow/shadow on dark backgrounds (mirroring gaming UI panels). Event cards, challenge cards, and profile cards should feel like in-game item cards.

### 7.2 Component Priorities

Build these reusable components first (in order):
1. **Event Card** -- used everywhere: Discord embeds (image), app feed, event listing, recaps.
2. **Challenge Card** -- enrollment, progress, completion states.
3. **XP Progress Bar** -- always visible in app header or profile.
4. **Consent Toggle** -- custom component: icon + label + description + toggle + "learn more" expandable.
5. **Leaderboard Row** -- rank, avatar, name, score, trend indicator.
6. **Badge** -- earned and locked states, with tooltip describing achievement.
7. **Stat Card** -- single metric display with gaming-themed comparison text.
8. **QR Scanner** -- full-screen camera view with overlay for event check-in.

## 9. Key Risks and Mitigations (Product/UX Perspective)

| Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|
| **Consent fatigue**: Users dismiss or ignore consent prompts, undermining data quality and legal compliance. | High | Progressive consent model (Section 5.1). Never ask for consent before delivering value. Make consent feel like a feature ("Desbloquea desafios de bienestar"), not a legal form. |
| **Discord-App gap**: Users stay in Discord and never migrate to the app, starving the data platform. | High | Bridge design (Section 5.2). App must offer value Discord cannot: XP tracking, challenges, wearable integration, event check-in, badges. Discord bot teases but never replicates. |
| **Gamification cringe**: Wellness gamification feels corporate/fake, alienating core gamers. | High | Gaming-native design (Section 5.3). Hire gamers, not wellness marketers, to design the system. Beta test with competitive gamers. If they mock it, redesign. |
| **Empty room problem**: App launches with no content, no events, no active challenges. | Medium | Seed with founding community from Discord. Soft launch app to first 50 members with direct feedback loop. Pre-populate challenge templates. Never show an empty leaderboard -- show "Se el primero en completar este desafio." |
| **Moderator burnout**: Mental health discussions in a community with 37% depression/anxiety rates create unsustainable emotional load on volunteer mods. | Medium | Mandatory rotation schedules. Clear escalation protocols. Partnership with licensed mental health provider. Mods are not therapists -- train on boundaries and referrals, not intervention. |
| **Low wearable adoption**: Many Ecuadorian gamers (especially mobile-first casual gamers) do not own wearables. | Medium | Manual entry as first-class citizen. Phone step counter (built into most smartphones) as baseline. Wearable integration as enhancement, not requirement. Partner with Tigo for subsidized fitness trackers as growth lever. |
| **Re-identification of "anonymized" data**: Small population (Ecuador gamers) makes true anonymization technically difficult. | High | Minimum cohort sizes (n>=50) enforced in B2B dashboard. Differential privacy techniques in analytics pipeline. Legal review of all data products before partner access. Architect review required. |
| **Partner expectations vs. reality**: Early B2B partners expect massive audience that doesn't exist yet. | Medium | Set explicit expectations in sales process. Lead with engagement quality metrics over audience size. Offer pilot pricing with growth commitments. The anchor partner (insurer) should be a co-creation partner, not a customer buying a commodity. |
